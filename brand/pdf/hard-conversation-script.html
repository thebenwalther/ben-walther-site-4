The Hard-Conversation Script.

Ben Walther

Four moves that keep the true thing sayable — and hearable. Draft it here, read it once out loud, then have the conversation while it's still uncomfortable.

01Frame: set the table

Why you're raising it, and what you're hoping for. The frame tells their nervous system this is an invitation, not an attack.

“I want to talk about something because the relationship matters to me, not because I'm angry.”
02Truth: say the actual thing

One clear sentence. Use “I,” describe the specific behavior, not their character. Specific is kind; vague is an ambush.

“When plans change at the last minute, I end up feeling like an afterthought.”
03Impact: make it human

Name the cost — to you, to them, to the thing you share. Not to guilt-trip; so the stakes are real.

“It's made me start holding back, and I don't want to be someone who holds back with you.”
04Ask: be concrete

A specific, doable request, not a vague hope. An ask gives them a way to say yes.

“Could we lock plans the day before instead of the hour of?”
